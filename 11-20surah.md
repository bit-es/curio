| **1. SURAH AD-DHUHA** | | | |
| Verse 1 | وَالضُّحَىٰ | Wad-dhuhaa | By the morning brightness |
| Verse 2 | وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا سَجَىٰ | Wal-laili idzaa sajaa | and [by] the night when it covers with darkness, |
| Verse 3 | مَا وَدَّعَكَ رَبُّكَ | Maa wadda'aka rabbuka | your Lord has not taken leave of you, |
| | وَمَا قَلَىٰ | wa maa qalaa | nor has He detested [you]. |
| Verse 4 | وَلَلْآخِرَةُ خَيْرٌ لَّكَ | Wa lal-aakhiratu khairul-laka | And the Hereafter is better for you |
| | مِنَ الْأُولَىٰ | minal-uulaa | than the first [life]. |
| Verse 5 | وَلَسَوْفَ يُعْطِيكَ رَبُّكَ | Wa lasaufa yu'thiika rabbuka | And your Lord is going to give you, |
| | فَتَرْضَىٰ | fatardhaa | and you will be satisfied. |
| Verse 6 | أَلَمْ يَجِدْكَ يَتِيمًا فَعَاوَىٰ | Alam yajidka yatiiman fa aawaa | Did He not find you an orphan and give [you] refuge? |
| Verse 7 | وَوَجَدَكَ ضَالًّا فَهَدَىٰ | Wa wajadaka dhaallan fahadaa | And He found you lost and guided [you], |
| Verse 8 | وَوَجَدَكَ عَائِلًا فَأَغْنَىٰ | Wa wajadaka 'aa'ilan fa aghnaa | and He found you poor and made [you] self-sufficient. |
| Verse 9 | فَأَمَّا الْيتِيمَ فَلَا تَقْهَرْ | Fa ammal-yatiima falaa taqhar | So as for the orphan, do not oppress [him]. |
| Verse 10 | وَأَمَّا السَّائِلَ فَلَا تَنْهَرْ | Wa ammas-saa'ila falaa tanhar | And as for the petitioner, do not repel [him]. |
| Verse 11 | وَأَمَّا بِنِعْمَةِ رَبِّكَ فَحَدِّثْ | Wa ammaa bini'mati rabbika fahaddits | And as for the favor of your Lord, report [it]. |

| **2. SURAH ASY-SYARH (AL-INSYIRAH)** | | | |
| Verse 1 | أَلَمْ نَشْرَحْ لَكَ صَدْرَكَ | Alam nasyrah laka shadrak | Did We not expand for you your breast? |
| Verse 2 | وَوَضَعْنَا عَنكَ وِزْرَكَ | Wa wadha'naa 'anka wizrak | And We removed from you your burden |
| Verse 3 | الَّذِي أَنقَضَ ظَهْرَكَ | Alladzii anqadha zhahrak | which weighed upon your back |
| Verse 4 | وَرَفَعْنَا لَكَ ذِكْرَكَ | Wa rafa'naa laka dzikrak | and raised high for you your repute. |
| Verse 5 | فَإِنَّ مَعَ الْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا | Fa inna ma'al-'usri yusraa | For indeed, with hardship [will be] ease. |
| Verse 6 | إِنَّ مَعَ الْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا | Inna ma'al-'usri yusraa | Indeed, with hardship [will be] ease. |
| Verse 7 | فَإِذَا فَرَغْتَ فَانصَبْ | Fa idzaa faraghta fanshab | So when you have finished, labor effortlessly [in worship]. |
| Verse 8 | وَإِلَىٰ رَبِّكَ فَارْغَب | Wa ilaa rabbika farghab | And to your Lord direct [your] longing. |

| **3. SURAH AT-TIN** | | | |
| Verse 1 | وَالتِّينِ وَالزَّيْتُونِ | Wat-tiini waz-zaituun | By the fig and the olive |
| Verse 2 | وَطُورِ سِينِينَ | Wa thuuri siiniin | and [by] Mount Sinai |
| Verse 3 | وَهَٰذَا الْبَلَدِ الْأَمِينِ | Wa haadzal-baladil-amiin | and [by] this secure city, |
| Verse 4 | لَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا الْإِنسَانَ | Laqad khalaqnal-insaana | We have certainly created man |
| | فِي أَحْسَنِ تَقْوِيمٍ | fii ahsani taqwiim | in the best of statures; |
| Verse 5 | ثُمَّ رَدَدْنَاهُ أَسْفَلَ سَافِلِينَ | Tsumma radadnaahu asfala saafiliin | then We return him to the lowest of the low, |
| Verse 6 | إِلَّا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا | Illal-ladziina aamanuu | except for those who believe |
| | وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ | wa 'amilush-shoolihaati | and do righteous deeds, |
| | فَلَهُمْ أَجْرٌ غَيْرُ مَمْنُونٍ | falahum ajrun ghairu mamnuun | for they will have a reward uninterrupted. |
| Verse 7 | فَمَا يُكَذِّبُكَ بَعْدُ بِالدِّينِ | Famaa yukadz-dzibuka ba'du bid-diin | So what yet causes you to deny the Recompense? |
| Verse 8 | أَلَيْسَ اللَّهُ بِأَحْكَمِ الْحَاكِمِينَ | Alaisal-laahu bi ahkamil-haakimiin | Is not Allah the most just of judges? |

| **4. SURAH AL-QADR** | | | |
| Verse 1 | إِنَّآ أَنزَلْنَٰهُ | Innaa anzalnaahu | Indeed, We sent it down |
| | فِي لَيْلَةِ الْقَدْرِ | fii lailatil-qadr | during the Night of Decree. |
| Verse 2 | وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا لَيْلَةُ الْقَدْرِ | Wa maa adraaka maa lailatul-qadr | And what can make you know what is the Night of Decree? |
| Verse 3 | لَيْلَةُ الْقَدْرِ خَيْرٌ | Lailatul-qadri khairum | The Night of Decree is better |
| | مِّنْ أَلْفِ شَهْرٍ | min alfi syahr | than a thousand months. |
| Verse 4 | تَنَزَّلُ الْمَلَٰٓئِكَةُ وَالرُّوحُ | Tanazzalul-malaa'ikatu war-ruuhu | The angels and the Spirit descend |
| | فِيهَا بِإِذْنِ رَبِّهِم | fiihaa bi idzni rabbihim | therein by permission of their Lord |
| | مِّن كُلِّ أَمْرٍ | min kulli amr | for every matter. |
| Verse 5 | سَلَٰمٌ هِيَ حَتَّىٰ مَطْلَعِ الْفَجْرِ | Salaamun hiya hattaa mathla'il-fajr | Peace it is until the emergence of dawn. |

| **5. SURAH AL-ZALZALAH** | | | |
| Verse 1 | إِذَا زُلْزِلَتِ الْأَرْضُ زِلْزَالَهَا | Idzaa zulzilatil-ardhu zilzaalahaa | When the earth is shaken with its [final] earthquake |
| Verse 2 | وَأَخْرَجَتِ الْأَرْضُ أَثْقَالَهَا | Wa akhrajatil-ardhu atsqaalahaa | and the earth discharges its burdens |
| Verse 3 | وَقَالَ الْإِنسَانُ مَا لَهَا | Wa qaalal-insaana maa lahaa | and man says, "What is [wrong] with it?" |
| Verse 4 | يَوْمَئِذٍ تُحَدِّثُ أَخْبَارَهَا | Yauma'idzin tuhadditsu akhbaarahaa | That Day, it will report its news |
| Verse 5 | بِأَنَّ رَبَّكَ أَوْحَىٰ لَهَا | Bi anna rabbaka auhaa lahaa | because your Lord has inspired it. |
| Verse 6 | يَوْمَئِذٍ يَصْدُرُ النَّاسُ أَشْتَاتًا | Yauma'idziy-yasdurun-naasu ashtaataa | That Day, the people will depart separated |
| | liyurau a'maalahum | to be shown their deeds. |
| Verse 7 | فَمَن يَعْمَلْ مِثْقَالَ ذَرَّةٍ خَيْرًا يَرَهُ | Famay-ya'mal mithqaala dzarratin khairay-yarah | So whoever does an atom's weight of good will see it, |
| Verse 8 | وَمَن يَعْمَلْ مِثْقَالَ ذَرَّةٍ شَرًّا يَرَهُ | Wa may-ya'mal mithqaala dzarratin syarray-yarah | and whoever does an atom's weight of evil will see it. |

| **6. SURAH AL-ADIYAT** | | | |
| Verse 1 | وَالْعَادِيَاتِ ضَبْحًا | Wal-'aadiyaati dhabhaa | By the racers, panting, |
| Verse 2 | فَالْمُورِيَاتِ قَدْحًا | Fal-muuriyaati qadhaa | and the producers of sparks [when] striking |
| Verse 3 | فَالْمُغِيرَاتِ صُبْحًا | Fal-mughiiraati shubhaa | and the raiders at dawn, |
| Verse 4 | فَأَثَرْنَ بِهِ نَقْعًا | Fa atsarna bihii naq'aa | stirring up thereby [clouds of] dust, |
| Verse 5 | فَوَسَطْنَ بِهِ جَمْعًا | Fawasathna bihii jam'aa | arriving thereby in the center collectively, |
| Verse 6 | إِنَّ الْإِنسَانَ لِرَبِّهِ لَكَنُودٌ | Innal-insaana lirabbihii lakanuud | indeed mankind, to his Lord, is ungrateful. |
| Verse 7 | وَإِنَّهُ عَلَىٰ ذَٰلِكَ لَشَهِيدٌ | Wa innahuu 'alaa dzaalika lasyahiid | And indeed, he is to that a witness. |
| Verse 8 | وَإِنَّهُ لِحُبِّ الْخَيْرِ لَشَدِيدٌ | Wa innahuu lihubbil-khairi lasyadiid | And indeed, he is intense in the love of wealth. |
| Verse 9 | أَفَلَا يَعْلَمُ إِذَا بُعْثِرَ | Afalaa ya'lamu idzaa bu'tsira | Does he not know that when is scattered |
| | مَا فِي الْقُبُورِ | maa fil-qubuur | what is in the graves |
| Verse 10 | وَحُصِّلَ مَا فِي الصُّدُورِ | Wa hush-shila maa fish-shudoor | and obtained is what is in the breasts, |
| Verse 11 | إِنَّ رَبَّهُم بِهِمْ يَوْمَئِذٍ لَّخَبِيرٌ | Inna rabbahum bihim yauma'idzil-lakhabiir | indeed, their Lord with them, that Day, is All-Aware. |

| **7. SURAH AL-QARIAH** | | | |
| Verse 1 | الْقَارِعَةُ | Al-qaari'ah | The Striking Calamity - |
| Verse 2 | مَا الْقَارِعَةُ | Mal-qaari'ah | what is the Striking Calamity? |
| Verse 3 | وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا الْقَارِعَةُ | Wa maa adraaka mal-qaari'ah | And what can make you know what is the Striking Calamity? |
| Verse 4 | يَوْمَ يَكُونُ النَّاسُ | Yauma yakuunun-naasu | It is the Day when mankind will be |
| | كَالْفَرَاشِ الْمَبْثُوثِ | kal-faraasyil-mabtsuuts | like moths dispersed, |
| Verse 5 | وَتَكُونُ الْجِبَالُ | Wa takuunul-jibaalu | and the mountains will be |
| | كَالْعِهْنِ الْمَنفُوشِ | kal-'ihnil-manfuusy | like wool, fluffed up. |
| Verse 6 | فَأَمَّا مَن ثَقُلَتْ مَوَازِينُهُ | Fa ammaa man tsaqulat mawaaziinuh | Then as for one whose scales are heavy [with good deeds], |
| Verse 7 | فَهُوَ فِي عِيشَةٍ رَّاضِيَةٍ | Fahuwa fii 'iisyatir-raadhiyah | he will be in a pleasant life. |
| Verse 8 | وَأَمَّا مَنْ خَفَّتْ مَوَازِينُهُ | Wa ammaa man khaffat mawaaziinuh | But as for one whose scales are light, |
| Verse 9 | فَأُمُّهُ هَاوِيَةٌ | Fa ummuhuu haawiyah | his refuge will be an abyss. |
| Verse 10 | وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا هِيَهْ | Wa maa adraaka maa hiyah | And what can make you know what that is? |
| Verse 11 | نَارٌ حَامِيَةٌ | Naarun haamiyah | It is a Fire, intensely hot. |

| **8. SURAH AT-TAKATHUR** | | | |
| Verse 1 | أَلْهَاكُمُ التَّكَاثُرُ | Alhaakumut-takaatsur | Competition in [worldly] increase diverts you |
| Verse 2 | حَتَّىٰ زُرْتُمُ الْمَقَابِرَ | Hattaa zurtumul-maqaabir | until you visit the graveyards. |
| Verse 3 | كَلَّا سَوْفَ تَعْلَمُونَ | Kallaa saufa ta'lamuun | No! You are going to know. |
| Verse 4 | ثُمَّ كَلَّا سَوْفَ تَعْلَمُونَ | Tsumma kallaa saufa ta'lamuun | Then no! You are going to know. |
| Verse 5 | كَلَّا لَوْ تَعْلَمُونَ عِلْمَ الْيَقِينِ | Kallaa lau ta'lamuuna 'ilmal-yaqiin | No! If you only knew with knowledge of certainty... |
| Verse 6 | لَتَرَوُنَّ الْجَحِيمَ | Latarawunnal-jahiim | You will surely see the Hellfire. |
| Verse 7 | ثُمَّ لَتَرَوُنَّهَا عَيْنَ الْيَقِينِ | Tsumma latarawunnahaa 'ainal-yaqiin | Then you will surely see it with the eye of certainty. |
| Verse 8 | ثُمَّ لَتُسْأَلُنَّ يَوْمَئِذٍ عَنِ النَّعِيمِ | Tsumma latus'alunna yauma'idzin 'anin-na'iim | Then you will surely be asked that Day about pleasure. |

| **9. SURAH AL-MASAD** | | | |
| Verse 1 | تَبَّتْ يَدَآ أَبِي لَهَبٍ وَتَبَّ | Tabbat yadaa abii lahabiw-watabb | May the hands of Abu Lahab be ruined, and ruined is he. |
| Verse 2 | مَآ أَغْنَىٰ عَنْهُ مَالُهُۥ وَمَا كَسَبَ | Maa aghnaa 'anhu maaluhuu wa maa kasab | His wealth will not avail him or that which he gained. |
| Verse 3 | سَيَصْلَىٰ نَارًا ذَاتَ لَهَبٍ | Sayashlaa naaran dzaata lahab | He will [enter to] burn in a Fire of [blazing] flame |
| Verse 4 | وَامْرَأَتُهُۥ حَمَّالَةَ الْحَطَبِ | Wamra'atuhuu hammaalatal-hatwab | and his wife [as well] - the carrier of firewood. |
| Verse 5 | فِي جِيدِهَا حَبْلٌ مِّن مَّسَدٍ | Fii jiidihaa hablum-mim-masad | Around her neck is a rope of [twisted] fiber. |
